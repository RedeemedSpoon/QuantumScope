Perturbation Theory

Perturbation theory is a powerful mathematical tool used in physics and mathematics to solve complex problems. It consists of finding an approximate solution to a difficult problem based on the solution of a simpler problem.

Disruption

A disruption is a small change to an otherwise well-understood system. This modification can be mathematically represented by a parameter ε (epsilon) assumed to be small.

Perturbation theory

Perturbation theory is a method for obtaining an approximate solution to a problem by breaking it into two parts:

  • An unperturbed problem (ε = 0): This is the initial system without the disturbance, the solution of which is assumed to be known.
  • A disturbance (ε ≠ 0): This is the small change made to the initial system.

Using the solution of the unperturbed problem as a starting point, the theory of disturbances makes it possible to obtain a series of successive approximations of the solution of the perturbed problem, as a function of the magnitude of the disturbance ε.

Unperturbed solution

Solution of the problem without the disturbance, denoted E₀ or ψ₀ depending on whether it is an eigenvalue (energy) or a wave function.

Disturbed solution

Solution of the problem with the disturbance, denoted E or ψ in general, which can be expressed as a series of powers of ε:

E = E₀ + εE₁ + ε²E₂ + ... ψ = ψ₀ + εψ₁ + ε²ψ₂ + ...

Perturbation theory is an important tool for finding approximate solutions to complex problems in many scientific fields. It is based on the idea that a difficult problem can often be understood by breaking it down into a simpler problem and considering a small change (perturbation) applied to it.

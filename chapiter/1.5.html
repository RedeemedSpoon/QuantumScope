Superposition & Entanglement

Imagine a coin that lands neither heads nor tails, but simultaneously exists in both states at once. This is the essence of superposition, a fascinating principle according to which a quantum particle can exist in several states simultaneously, until it is measured and it "chooses" a single state.

This concept may seem strange, but it has been repeatedly validated by experiments, such as the double slit experiment with electrons. Passing through the slits, the electron behaves like a wave, exploring all possible paths simultaneously, before "materializing" into a single point on the screen upon detection.

Entanglement is an even stranger and counterintuitive phenomenon. Imagine two coins linked in a mysterious way, so that if one lands heads, the other automatically lands tails, and vice versa, even if they are separated by a great distance. This is the essence of quantum entanglement: two particles, once entangled, share an instantaneous link, and measuring the state of one instantly determines the state of the other, regardless of distance. that separates them.

Entanglement has profound implications for understanding the nature of reality and could revolutionize the field of communication and quantum computing. However, it does not allow information to be transmitted faster than light, because it cannot send any signal. It's instant correlation, but not instant communication.

Superposition and entanglement are key concepts in quantum mechanics that challenge our intuition and open the way to revolutionary applications. Although these notions seem paradoxical, they have been validated experimentally and force us to review our understanding of reality on a microscopic scale.

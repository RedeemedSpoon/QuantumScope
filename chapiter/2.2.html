Periodic Table & Chemical Bonding

The periodic table is an essential tool for understanding chemical bonding. It organizes elements based on their atomic number, which is the number of protons in an atom's nucleus. An element's position on the periodic table gives us valuable information about its chemical properties, including its tendency to form bonds with other elements.

The elements of the periodic table

  • Metals: Metals are generally found on the left side of the periodic table. They tend to lose electrons during chemical reactions and have a positive oxidation number.
  • Nonmetals: Nonmetals are usually found on the right side of the periodic table. They tend to gain electrons during chemical reactions and have a negative oxidation number.
  • Metalloids: Metalloids lie on the boundary between metals and non-metals. They can exhibit properties of both groups.
  • Noble gases: Noble gases are found on the far right of the periodic table. They are very stable and tend not to react with other elements.

Chemical bond

  • Ionic bond: An ionic bond is formed when an atom donates one or more electrons to another atom. This creates oppositely charged ions that are attracted to each other by electrostatic force.
  • Covalent bond: A covalent bond forms when two atoms share electrons. This creates an attractive force between the nuclei of the atoms and the shared electrons.
  • Metallic bond: A metallic bond forms when the valence electrons of several atoms are delocalized and form an electron cloud. This creates an attractive force between the atom nuclei and the electron cloud.

The type of chemical bond

  • Atoms that are in the same group on the periodic table tend to form covalent bonds with each other.
  • Atoms that are in different groups on the periodic table tend to form ionic bonds with each other.
  • Metals tend to form metallic bonds with each other.

Predict chemical bond type

  • If the two atoms are in the same group, they are likely to form a covalent bond.
  • If the two atoms are in different groups, and the difference in atomic number is large, they are likely to form an ionic bond.
  • If both atoms are metals, they are likely to form a metallic bond.

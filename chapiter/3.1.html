Linear Algebra & Bra-ket Notation

Linear algebra is a branch of mathematics that studies vector spaces and linear transformations. It plays a crucial role in quantum physics, making it possible to describe the quantum states of physical systems.

Fundamental Concepts of Linear Algebra

Vector spaces

A vector space is a set E on which we can define two operations:

  • Vector addition: which to two vectors u and v of E associates a third vector u + v of E.
  • Multiplication by a scalar: which associates a vector λu of E with a scalar λ and a vector u of E.

These operations must satisfy certain properties, such as the commutativity and associativity of vector addition, the distributivity of multiplication by a scalar with respect to vector addition and multiplication by another scalar.

Properties of Vector Spaces

  • Dimension: A vector space can have finite or infinite dimension. The dimension represents the minimum number of linearly independent vectors necessary to generate the entire space.
  • Bases: A basis of a vector space is a set of linearly independent vectors that generates the entire vector space.
  • Linear transformations: A linear transformation is a map f between two vector spaces E and F which preserves vector structures, that is to say:
    • f(u + v) = f(u) + f(v) for all vectors u and v of E.
    • f(λu) = λf(u) for all scalars λ and all vectors u of E.

Applications of Linear Algebra

Linear algebra is used in many fields, including physics, engineering, computer science, economics and finance.

Bra-ket notation

The bra-ket notation is a convention used in quantum physics to represent vectors in a vector space and linear transformations.

  • Ket: A ket symbolizes a vector of a vector space. It is usually represented by a vertical symbol followed by a letter or group of letters, for example |ψ⟩.
  • Bra: A bra is the conjugated transpose of a ket. It is usually represented by a horizontal symbol followed by a letter or group of letters in parentheses, for example ⟨φ|.
  • Dot product: The dot product of two kets |ψ⟩ and |φ⟩ is a complex number, denoted ⟨φ|ψ⟩.
  • Linear transformation: A linear transformation is represented by a matrix. The action of the transformation on a ket |ψ⟩ is denoted by A|ψ⟩.

Bra-ket Notation Properties

  • The dot product of two kets is linear in each argument.
  • The dot product of a ket with itself is positive.
  • The dot product of two kets is zero if and only if the kets are orthogonal.

Applications of Bra-ket Notation

  • Quantum mechanics: Description of the quantum states of particles.
  • Quantum electromagnetism: Description of the interactions between photons and electromagnetic fields.
  • Quantum field theory: Description of interactions between particles.

Linear algebra and bra-ket notation are essential tools for quantum physics. They make it possible to describe the quantum states of physical systems and to study their evolution.
